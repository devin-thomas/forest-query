What The Analysis Found

World forest area fell from 41.28 million square kilometers in 1990 to 39.96 million in 2016. The loss is roughly comparable to the 2016 land area of Peru, which gives the scale of the decline a concrete real-world frame of reference.

Latin America & Caribbean remained the most forested region in both 1990 and 2016, but it also posted the largest regional drop. Sub-Saharan Africa was the only other non-world region to lose forest share over the same period.

Where ForestQuery Should Focus

  • Prioritize Brazil and Indonesia because of their exceptionally large absolute losses.
  • Prioritize Nigeria because it appears in the top five for both absolute and percent decline.
  • Study China and Iceland as positive outliers for possible reforestation lessons.
  • Concentrate regional attention on Latin America & Caribbean and Sub-Saharan Africa.
